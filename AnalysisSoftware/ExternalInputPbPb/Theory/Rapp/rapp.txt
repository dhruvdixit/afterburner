                    
 Direct photon spectra, E dN_gamma/d^3p, and ellipitc flow, v2, for 2.76TeV Pb-Pb 
 collisions at various centralities (0-20%, 20-40% and 40-80%) based on the paper
 H. van Hees, M. He, R. Rapp, NPA933(2015)256. 
 An elliptic thermal fireball evolution is employed for the bulk medium (with 
 lQCD-based equation of state [He et al. PRC85(2012)044911] above Tc=170MeV and 
 hadro-chemical freezeout below T_ch=160MeV).
 Given below is the in-medium radiation from hadronic matter (in-medium rho 
 spectral function and additional meson-gas sources from t-channel meson exchange 
 and pi-pi + pi-K Bremsstrahlung) and QGP (AMY rates).
 Short-lived final-state decays are accounted for, long-lived ones not.  
 Non-thermal primordial photons from binary NN collisions are estimated from
 a pQCD-motivated xt-scaling ansatz; their v2 is assumed to be zero. 

 Column legend for spectra tables:
 1. column: photon transverse momentum 
 2. column: hadronic thermal radiation from in-medium rho spectral function
 3.   "   : QGP thermal radiation
 4.   "   : hadronic thermal radiatin from omega t-channel exchange in pi+rho->pi+gamma
 5.   "   : hadronic thermal radiation from meson gas including pi-pi and pi-K Bremsstrahlung
 6.   "   : primordial photons from pQCD motivated xt-scaling
 7.   "   : sum of columns 2+3+4+5+6

 Column legend for v2 tables:
 1. column: photon transverse momentum
 2. column: v2 from in-medium rho spectral function source
 3.   "   : QGP 
 4.   "   : omega t-channel in pi+rho->pi+gamma
 5.   "   : meson gas including pi-pi and pi-K Bremsstrahlung
 6.   "   : omega-t-channel + meson-gas (weighted sum of 4 and 5)  
 7.   "   : weighted sum of total thermal and primordial
 8.   "   : weighted sum of all thermal sources 


 Direct questions or comments to:  rapp@comp.tamu.edu, hees@fias.uni-frankfurt.de, minhephys@gmail.com
 (Texas A&M Sept. 2015)
